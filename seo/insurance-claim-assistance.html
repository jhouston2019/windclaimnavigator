Why Most Policyholders Need Assistance

The insurance claim process is intentionally complex. Carriers have refined their systems over decades to create information asymmetry—they know exactly how claims work, and you don't.

Inadequate Initial Offers

The first estimate you receive is almost always low. Adjusters use software that systematically undervalues materials and labor. They apply aggressive depreciation that reduces your payout. They exclude items that should be covered. Most policyholders don't know enough to challenge these numbers effectively.

Documentation Traps

Insurance companies will ask for extensive documentation, but they rarely tell you exactly what they need or in what format. Submit the wrong evidence, and your claim gets delayed or denied. Miss a deadline, and you lose your right to appeal.

Policy Language Complexity

Your insurance policy is a legal contract written in dense language that's difficult for non-lawyers to interpret. Adjusters will cite specific policy provisions to justify denials or limitations. Without understanding what your policy actually says, you can't effectively argue for proper coverage.
