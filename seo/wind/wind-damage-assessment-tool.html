Professional Wind Damage Assessment

Properly assessing wind damage is the foundation of a successful insurance claim. Missing damage or failing to document it correctly can result in denied claims or settlements that don't cover full repair costs. Wind Claim Navigator provides systematic assessment tools that help you identify, document, and report all wind damage to your property.

Our wind damage assessment approach is based on industry standards used by professional adjusters and contractors, ensuring you capture the same level of detail that insurance companies expect.

Why Comprehensive Assessment Matters

  • Hidden damage: Wind damage isn't always visible from the ground; systematic assessment reveals all affected areas
  • Consequential damage: Wind often causes secondary damage that must be documented for full coverage
  • Claim completeness: Supplemental claims for missed damage are harder to get approved
  • Settlement accuracy: Complete assessment ensures repair estimates cover all necessary work
  • Denial prevention: Thorough documentation prevents insurers from claiming insufficient evidence

What Wind Damage Assessment Includes

A comprehensive wind damage assessment evaluates all property components:

  • Roof system: Shingles, flashing, ridge caps, vents, and structural components
  • Exterior walls: Siding, trim, soffit, fascia, and wall structure
  • Windows and doors: Glass, frames, screens, and seals
  • Gutters and downspouts: Attachment, integrity, and functionality
  • Fences and gates: Posts, panels, and hardware
  • Detached structures: Garages, sheds, carports, and pergolas
  • Trees and landscaping: Damage to or from vegetation
  • Interior damage: Water intrusion, ceiling damage, and related issues

How to Conduct a Wind Damage Assessment

Wind Claim Navigator guides you through a systematic assessment process:

Step 1: Safety First

Before beginning your assessment:

  • Ensure the property is safe to inspect (no downed power lines, structural instability)
  • Don't climb on damaged roofs or structures
  • Use binoculars or zoom cameras for elevated areas
  • Document hazards and emergency conditions first

Step 2: Exterior Assessment

Systematically inspect all exterior components:

  • Walk the perimeter of your property, photographing from all angles
  • Document each elevation (front, back, sides) separately
  • Look for missing, damaged, or displaced materials
  • Note any exposed underlayment or structural components
  • Photograph damage from multiple distances (wide shots and close-ups)
  • Document the extent and pattern of damage

Step 3: Interior Assessment

Check for interior damage resulting from wind:

  • Look for water stains on ceilings and walls
  • Check attic spaces for roof penetration and water intrusion
  • Document any interior damage to contents or finishes
  • Note the relationship between exterior damage and interior effects

Step 4: Documentation

Create comprehensive records of all damage:

  • Take photos and videos with timestamps
  • Create a written inventory of all damaged items and areas
  • Measure and note the extent of damage (square footage, linear footage)
  • Collect any fallen materials as physical evidence
  • Obtain weather reports documenting wind speeds
