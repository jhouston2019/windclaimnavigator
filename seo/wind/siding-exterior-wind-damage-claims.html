Understanding Exterior Wind Damage Claims

Wind damage to your home's exterior goes beyond just the roof. High winds can damage siding, fences, gutters, windows, doors, and other exterior components. These damages are typically covered under homeowners insurance wind damage provisions, but insurers often minimize or dispute the extent of exterior damage claims.

Wind Claim Navigator helps you document all exterior wind damage comprehensively, ensuring nothing is overlooked and your claim reflects the full scope of repairs needed.

Common Types of Exterior Wind Damage

  • Siding damage: Torn, cracked, or missing vinyl, wood, or fiber cement siding
  • Fence damage: Blown-down sections, leaning posts, or broken panels
  • Gutter damage: Detached, bent, or torn gutters and downspouts
  • Window damage: Broken glass, damaged frames, or torn screens from wind-blown debris
  • Door damage: Damaged garage doors, entry doors, or storm doors
  • Soffit and fascia: Torn or missing soffit panels and fascia boards
  • Trim and molding: Damaged exterior trim, corner boards, or decorative elements
  • Outdoor structures: Damaged sheds, pergolas, decks, or carports
  • Landscaping: Downed trees, damaged plants, or erosion (coverage varies)

Why Exterior Wind Damage Claims Get Challenged

Insurance companies frequently dispute exterior wind damage claims for several reasons:

  • Cosmetic vs. functional: Insurers may claim damage is only cosmetic and doesn't require repair
  • Age and condition: Arguing that damage is from wear and tear, not wind
  • Improper installation: Suggesting poor installation caused failure rather than wind force
  • Lack of maintenance: Claiming neglect contributed to damage severity
  • Coverage limits: Applying lower limits for fences, detached structures, or landscaping
  • Causation disputes: Questioning whether wind actually caused the damage

Documenting Siding and Exterior Wind Damage

Thorough documentation is essential for exterior wind damage claims. Wind Claim Navigator guides you through the evidence-gathering process:

  • Photograph all damaged exterior components from multiple angles
  • Document the full extent of siding damage, including hidden areas
  • Capture close-up images showing how wind caused the damage
  • Photograph fence damage including leaning posts and broken panels
  • Document gutter detachment points and damage patterns
  • Record any wind-blown debris that caused impact damage
  • Take before photos if available to show pre-damage condition
  • Obtain weather reports confirming high winds in your area
  • Get professional estimates from licensed contractors for all repairs
  • Document temporary repairs and associated costs

Maximizing Your Exterior Wind Damage Settlement

Many homeowners receive settlements that only cover partial exterior repairs, leaving them to pay out-of-pocket for complete restoration. Wind Claim Navigator helps you avoid this outcome by:

  • Identifying all damaged components, not just the most obvious ones
  • Documenting functional impacts, not just cosmetic issues
  • Verifying that adjuster estimates include all necessary repairs
  • Preparing appeals when initial settlements are insufficient
  • Understanding policy coverage for different exterior components
  • Tracking all claim communications and deadlines

Special Considerations for Different Materials

Different siding and exterior materials require different documentation approaches:

  • Vinyl siding: Document cracks, holes, and missing pieces; note if matching replacement is available
  • Wood siding: Show splitting, splintering, or complete removal; document water intrusion risk
  • Fiber cement: Photograph cracks and chips; note if damage compromises weather resistance
  • Stucco: Document cracks and separation; show if damage allows water penetration
  • Brick/stone: Show mortar damage, cracks, or displaced materials
