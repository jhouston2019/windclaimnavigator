Understanding Wind Damage Coverage

Most standard homeowners insurance policies include coverage for wind damage, but understanding exactly what is covered, how much coverage you have, and what exclusions apply is essential for successfully navigating a wind damage claim.

Wind Claim Navigator helps you analyze your policy coverage, identify potential coverage gaps, and ensure you're claiming all covered damages from your wind event.

What Standard Homeowners Insurance Typically Covers

Standard homeowners insurance policies (HO-3) generally provide coverage for wind damage to:

  • Dwelling: Your home's structure, including roof, walls, foundation, and attached structures
  • Other structures: Detached garages, sheds, fences, and other structures on your property (usually 10% of dwelling coverage)
  • Personal property: Contents damaged by wind or subsequent water intrusion (usually 50-70% of dwelling coverage)
  • Additional living expenses: Temporary housing if your home is uninhabitable due to wind damage
  • Debris removal: Cost to remove fallen trees, damaged materials, and other debris

Specific Wind Damage Typically Covered

  • Roof damage from high winds (shingle blow-off, uplift, structural damage)
  • Siding damage or removal from wind force
  • Window and door damage from wind or wind-blown debris
  • Fence damage or destruction from high winds
  • Gutter and downspout damage
  • Soffit, fascia, and trim damage
  • Damage to attached structures (porches, decks, carports)
  • Interior damage from wind-driven rain entering through wind-damaged openings
  • Tree damage to structures from wind-toppled trees
  • Damage to personal property from wind or resulting water intrusion

Important Coverage Limitations and Exclusions

While wind damage is generally covered, several important limitations and exclusions can affect your claim:

Common Wind Coverage Limitations

  • Wind/hail deductibles: Many policies have separate, higher deductibles for wind damage (often 1-5% of dwelling coverage)
  • Cosmetic damage exclusions: Some policies exclude purely cosmetic damage that doesn't affect function
  • Detached structure limits: Coverage for fences, sheds, and detached garages is typically limited to 10% of dwelling coverage
  • Tree and landscaping limits: Usually limited to $500-$1,000 per tree, $5,000 total
  • Code upgrade limits: Coverage for building code upgrades may be limited or excluded
  • Matching limitations: Some policies limit coverage for matching undamaged sections

What's Typically NOT Covered

  • Pre-existing damage: Damage that existed before the wind event
  • Wear and tear: Damage from aging, deterioration, or lack of maintenance
  • Improper installation: Damage resulting from poor workmanship or installation
  • Neglect: Damage that could have been prevented with proper maintenance
  • Flood damage: Water damage from flooding requires separate flood insurance
  • Earth movement: Damage from landslides or ground shifting (even if caused by wind)
  • Power failure: Damage from power outages unless caused by direct physical damage to your property
  • Mold: Mold growth may be excluded or have limited coverage

Special Considerations for Wind Coverage

Coastal Properties and Hurricane Deductibles

If you live in a coastal area, your policy may include special provisions:

  • Separate hurricane deductibles (often 2-5% of dwelling coverage)
  • Hurricane deductible triggers (e.g., when National Weather Service declares a hurricane)
  • Potential exclusions for certain types of wind damage in coastal zones
  • Requirements for specific wind mitigation features

Replacement Cost vs. Actual Cash Value

Your policy's valuation method significantly affects your wind damage settlement:

  • Replacement Cost: Pays to replace damaged property with new materials of similar quality (no depreciation)
  • Actual Cash Value: Pays replacement cost minus depreciation for age and wear
  • Recoverable depreciation: With replacement cost coverage, you may receive ACV initially and recover depreciation after repairs are completed
