Why Insurance Claims Go Wrong

Filing an insurance claim should be straightforward, but most policyholders quickly discover the process is designed to frustrate, delay, and minimize payouts. When your home or property is damaged, you need help navigating a system built to protect the insurance company's bottom line—not your recovery.

Lowball Initial Offers

The first estimate you receive is almost always low. Adjusters use software that systematically undervalues materials and labor. They apply aggressive depreciation that reduces your payout. They exclude items that should be covered. Most policyholders don't know enough to challenge these numbers effectively.

Delayed Responses

Insurance companies keep asking for more documentation without clearly explaining what's needed. Each request adds days or weeks to the process. The longer your claim takes, the more likely you are to accept a low offer just to move forward with repairs.

Partial Denials

Even when claims aren't denied outright, insurance companies exclude legitimate damage items. They cite policy provisions without explaining them fully. They apply exclusions that don't actually apply to your situation.

Missing Scope Items

Adjusters routinely miss damage during inspections. They don't access all affected areas. They overlook secondary damage. They don't recognize problems that aren't immediately obvious. Missing scope items can cost thousands or tens of thousands of dollars.

What Insurance Companies Do

Behind the scenes, carriers use a playbook designed to minimize every claim.

  • Assign adjusters with claim-closing quotas and performance metrics tied to low payouts
  • Use proprietary software that systematically undervalues damage
  • Request excessive documentation to create delays and frustration
  • Apply aggressive depreciation schedules that reduce replacement costs
  • Deny coverage for items that should be included under your policy
  • Count on policyholders accepting the first offer out of exhaustion
