## Document Generator Details

### 1. Carrier Submission Cover Letter Generator

**When to Use:** Before submitting your complete claim package to the insurance company

**What It Does:** Creates a professional cover letter that:
- Introduces your claim package
- Lists all included documents
- States your total claim amount
- Provides contact information
- Sets professional tone for your submission

**Key Fields:**
- Insurance company name
- Policy and claim numbers
- Date and type of loss
- List of all documents you're submitting
- Total claim amount

**Pro Tip:** Be thorough when listing documents - this creates a record of everything you submitted.

---

### 2. Coverage Clarification Letter

**When to Use:** When policy language is unclear or you need the insurer to confirm coverage

**What It Does:** Requests written clarification on:
- Specific policy provisions
- Coverage interpretations
- Endorsement applications
- Sublimits and exclusions

**Key Fields:**
- Policy section in question
- Exact policy language
- Your interpretation
- What clarification you need

**Pro Tip:** Quote the exact policy language word-for-word to ensure you're discussing the same provision.

---

### 3. Estimate Revision Request Generator

**When to Use:** When the insurance company's estimate is too low or missing items

**What It Does:** Formally requests revisions to the adjuster's estimate by:
- Identifying specific discrepancies
- Documenting omitted items
- Challenging pricing
- Providing supporting evidence

**Key Fields:**
- Original estimate details
- Specific problems with estimate
- Your supporting evidence
- Requested revised amount

**Pro Tip:** Be specific about each discrepancy - vague complaints are easier to dismiss.

---

### 4. EUO Sworn Statement Guide

**When to Use:** When preparing for an Examination Under Oath (EUO)

**What It Does:** Creates a preparation guide that:
- Outlines what to expect
- Lists key facts to remember
- Identifies documents to review
- Highlights potential concern areas
- Provides best practices

**Key Fields:**
- EUO date and location
- Key facts about your claim
- Complex issues to address
- Documents to review beforehand

**Pro Tip:** Review this guide multiple times before your EUO. Being prepared shows credibility.

---

### 5. Follow-Up Status Letter

**When to Use:** When the insurance company hasn't responded within a reasonable timeframe

**What It Does:** Professionally requests status update on:
- Pending claim decisions
- Outstanding estimate reviews
- Delayed payments
- Unanswered questions

**Key Fields:**
- When you submitted claim/documents
- Days since submission
- What you're waiting for
- State deadlines (if applicable)

**Pro Tip:** Send this after 10-14 days of no response. Document all follow-ups.

---

### 6. Policy Interpretation Letter

**When to Use:** When you and the insurer disagree on what the policy means

**What It Does:** Documents your interpretation with:
- Exact policy language
- Your interpretation and reasoning
- Supporting authority (case law, regulations)
- Requested action

**Key Fields:**
- Policy section and language
- Your interpretation
- Insurer's position (if stated)
- Supporting authority
- What you want them to do

**Pro Tip:** This creates a paper trail if you need to escalate or file a complaint later.

---

### 7. Submission Confirmation Email

**When to Use:** Immediately after submitting your claim package

**What It Does:** Confirms submission and:
- Documents what was sent
- When it was sent
- How it was sent
- Tracking information
- Requested response timeframe

**Key Fields:**
- Submission date and method
- Tracking number
- Complete list of documents
- When you expect a response

**Pro Tip:** Send this even if you used certified mail - it creates an email record too.

---

### 8. Supplement Cover Letter Generator

**When to Use:** When submitting additional claim items after your initial claim

**What It Does:** Explains your supplemental claim by:
- Referencing original claim
- Explaining why supplement is needed
- Listing new documentation
- Calculating revised total

**Key Fields:**
- Original claim details
- Reason for supplement
- New items or corrections
- Revised total amount

**Pro Tip:** Clearly explain why items weren't in original claim to avoid suspicion.

## Sending Your Documents

### Recommended Methods:

1. **Certified Mail, Return Receipt Requested**
   - Creates legal proof of delivery
   - Shows exactly when insurer received it
   - Costs ~$8-10
   - **Best for:** Important deadlines, formal demands

2. **Email with Read Receipt**
   - Fast delivery
   - Electronic record
   - Request read receipt
   - **Best for:** Follow-ups, quick communications

3. **Insurance Company Portal**
   - Immediate confirmation
   - Integrated with claim file
   - Take screenshots of submission
   - **Best for:** Document uploads, routine submissions

4. **Fax with Confirmation**
   - Immediate delivery
   - Confirmation page proves sending
   - **Best for:** Deadline situations, when email unavailable

### Always Keep:
- Copy of document sent
- Proof of delivery/submission
- Date and time sent
- Method of delivery
- Tracking numbers
