Why deadlines matter

Deadlines control leverage, determine penalties, and can reset or shorten when evidence is late. Carrier clocks and state statutory clocks often differ—knowing both lets you push back on unreasonable demands and escalate when timelines slip.

State-level deadlines overview

States layer their own clocks on top of your policy. They dictate when carriers must acknowledge, investigate, decide, and pay. Knowing the local rules keeps pressure on the carrier and prevents avoidable delays.

  • 10–15 day acknowledgment standards
  • 30-day acceptance or denial timelines
  • Payment windows that range from 10–20 days
  • Reasonableness standards for document requests

What to do right now

  1. Add all known deadlines into the Claim Journal.
  2. Identify and correct missing evidence.
  3. Submit a timeline confirmation to the carrier.
  4. Request acknowledgment if overdue.
  5. Begin a follow-up schedule.
