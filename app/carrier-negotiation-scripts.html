First Notice of Loss (FNOL)

Launch your claim with clear expectations, deadlines, and documentation requirements.

Phone Script

“Hi [Adjuster Name], I’m opening my claim reported on [date]. Please confirm the claim number, your best contact, and the expected inspection date. I need the full list of documents you require and the statutory timeline for your coverage decision. I’ll email a summary after this call.”

Email Script

Subject: FNOL Confirmation — Claim #[claim number]

“Thank you for opening my claim on [date]. Please confirm: assigned adjuster contact, required documentation checklist, and target date for initial inspection and determination. I will provide requested items promptly and expect acknowledgment upon receipt.”

Follow-Up Script

“Following my FNOL on [date], please confirm inspection scheduling and the list of documents you require. I want to ensure timelines remain on track and that all communications are documented.”

Adjuster Scheduling

Lock in dates, scope, and expectations before the site visit.

Phone Script

“Hi [Adjuster Name], I need to confirm the inspection date, arrival window, and whether you’ll bring specialists for roofing, interiors, and contents. Please confirm you will review all damaged areas identified and capture photos for the file.”

Email Script

Subject: Inspection Confirmation

“Please confirm the scheduled inspection on [date/time], the scope to be reviewed, and that all structural and interior areas will be documented. Include your contact during the visit for any immediate clarifications.”

Follow-Up Script

“Confirming tomorrow’s inspection: arrival window [time], scope (roof, exterior, interior, contents), and that you’ll provide your notes or summary afterward. Please reply to confirm so we remain aligned.”

Documentation Requests

Control the record by confirming what was sent, when, and how it will be used.

Phone Script

“I’m submitting the documents you requested: [list]. Please confirm these meet your needs and identify any remaining items. What is the expected review completion date once these are in your file?”

Email Script

Subject: Documentation Submission — Claim #[claim number]

“Attached are the requested documents: [list]. Please confirm receipt, note any outstanding items, and provide the timeline for incorporating these into your evaluation. I will maintain this thread as proof of delivery.”

Follow-Up Script

“Checking in on the documents delivered on [date]. Please confirm they are in the claim file, whether anything further is needed, and the date you expect to finish review.”

Repair Estimate Disputes

Challenge omissions and require line-item responses to keep revisions moving.

Phone Script

“I reviewed your estimate dated [date]. Please identify, line by line, why items in my contractor’s scope (attached) were excluded. I need written responses with supporting measurements and photos so we can reconcile quickly.”

Email Script

Subject: Estimate Reconciliation Request

“Your estimate omits the following items: [list]. Please provide line-item justification, code references if applicable, and your proposed corrections. I will supply any additional documentation you need within 2 business days; please target a revised estimate by [date].”

Follow-Up Script

“Following up on the reconciliation sent on [date]. Please provide your line-item responses and the date the corrected estimate will be issued so payment timing stays on track.”

Valuation & Depreciation

Ensure pricing is accurate and that recoverable depreciation is scheduled for release.

Phone Script

“Please explain the pricing source and date you used for materials and labor. For depreciation, confirm what’s recoverable, what proof you need, and the date you will issue the depreciation payment after invoices are submitted.”

Email Script

Subject: Valuation Clarification

“Provide the pricing database and publication date used for this estimate, plus the formula and percentage applied for depreciation. Please confirm recoverable depreciation requirements and the payment release timeline upon proof of completion.”

Follow-Up Script

“I need written confirmation of the valuation source, depreciation percentages, and the date you will issue recoverable depreciation after I submit completion proof. Please respond today so work scheduling stays aligned with payment.”

Coverage Clarification

Require policy citations and clear rationale for any limitation or denial.

Phone Script

“Please cite the exact policy language you are relying on for this decision. I need the page, section, and paragraph plus your written interpretation and how it applies to my documented damages.”

Email Script

Subject: Coverage Rationale Request

“Provide the policy citation (page/section) supporting your position, along with how you applied it to the facts and documentation I supplied. Include any exclusions you are invoking and how you confirmed they apply.”

Follow-Up Script

“I still need the written policy citations and rationale for your coverage position. Please provide this today so I can evaluate next steps and avoid further delay.”

Delays & Non-Response

Reinforce deadlines, request status, and escalate lack of movement.

Phone Script

“I’m calling because the response due on [date] is outstanding. Please provide the current status and the exact date you will deliver the update. I’ll document this call in writing.”

Email Script

Subject: Response Required — Past Due

“Per our prior exchange on [date], your update was due by [deadline]. Please provide the status and the new committed date today. If we cannot meet that, I will escalate to your supervisor to keep the claim compliant with timelines.”

Follow-Up Script

“This is a second request for the update due on [date]. Please respond today with status and a firm delivery date. If no response, I will elevate to your supervisor for resolution.”

Undervaluation / Lowball

Press for evidence behind reductions and require corrected valuations.

Phone Script

“Your valuation is lower than market pricing and contractor bids. Please provide the pricing source, measurements, and photos used. I need your written explanation for each reduction and when you will issue the corrected valuation.”

Email Script

Subject: Valuation Review Request

“The offered amount does not align with documented bids and materials. Please provide the line-item rationale for reductions, the pricing database and date, and when you will reissue payment reflecting accurate market rates.”

Follow-Up Script

“Checking on the valuation review requested on [date]. Provide your written justifications and the date the corrected payment will be released so project scheduling remains intact.”

Reinspection Requests

Secure a second look with clear scope and documentation to resolve disputes.

Phone Script

“I’m requesting reinspection to review overlooked items documented on [date/photos]. Please confirm a date, the attending adjuster, and that the scope includes [areas]. I will provide access and contractor notes for efficiency.”

Email Script

Subject: Reinspection Request

“Please schedule reinspection for the documented items attached. Confirm the date/time, participants, and that all disputed line items will be reviewed with measurements and photos. I expect the revised estimate within [X] business days of the visit.”

Follow-Up Script

“Following up on my reinspection request sent [date]. Please provide the scheduled date and confirm the disputed items to be addressed so we avoid further delay.”

Supervisor Escalation

Escalate stalled issues with concise documentation and desired resolutions.

Phone Script

“I need a supervisor review because the file lacks timely responses on [issues]. Please connect me with your supervisor today and provide their contact. I will send a brief summary for prompt action.”

Email Script

Subject: Supervisor Review Request

“Please escalate to your supervisor regarding: [issue list]. I am requesting review within 2 business days and a written response outlining next steps and timelines. Attachments summarize the outstanding items.”

Follow-Up Script

“Checking on supervisor escalation requested [date]. Please provide the supervisor’s contact, the scheduled review date, and when I will receive a written response addressing each issue.”

Claims Manager Escalation

Escalate formally with clear defects, evidence, and requested remedies.

Phone Script

“The issues remain unresolved after supervisor review. I need a claims manager to address documented delays/omissions on [items]. Please provide their contact and commit to a review date this week.”

Email Script

Subject: Claims Manager Escalation

“I am escalating to a claims manager because of unresolved issues: [list]. Attached are timelines, prior requests, and evidence. I am requesting: (1) written responses to each item, (2) corrected estimate/payment dates, and (3) confirmation of compliance with claim handling timelines.”

Follow-Up Script

“Following up on the manager escalation sent [date]. Please provide the review status, who is assigned, and the date I will receive written resolution for each documented issue.”

DOI Complaint Scripts

State regulatory concerns and invite resolution before filing a formal complaint.

Phone Script

“I need to address compliance concerns before I file with the Department of Insurance. The file shows delays and incomplete responses on [items]. Please provide a manager who can resolve these within [X] business days.”

Email Script

Subject: Request to Cure — Pending DOI Complaint

“I intend to file a DOI complaint for delayed responses and unresolved items: [list]. Before filing, I am requesting: (1) written explanations for each delay, (2) corrected estimate/payment dates, and (3) confirmation of compliance steps. Please respond within [X] business days.”

Follow-Up Script

“This is a final request before submitting a DOI complaint. Please provide the written responses and dates to cure the issues identified on [date]. If no response, I will proceed with the filing.”

Common Carrier Responses

  • “We need more time.”
  • “Your estimate is too high.”
  • “We can’t open a supplement yet.”
  • “We don’t see evidence of that damage.”
  • “We need additional documentation.”
  • “Your contractor is overpriced.”
  • “We’ve concluded our position.”

Counter Scripts

“Statutory/contract timelines require updates by [date]. Please confirm your delivery date today so we remain compliant.”

“Provide the pricing source and publication date used. If you dispute my contractor’s rates, supply your alternative bids in writing.”

“The file supports a supplement with documented scope gaps. Please open the supplement and provide the target date for a revised estimate.”

“See attached photos and inspection notes confirming damage. If you disagree, identify the specific evidence you are relying on.”

“List the exact documents outstanding and how they impact your decision. I will submit them promptly with written acknowledgment of receipt.”

“If you believe pricing is high, show competitive bids you obtained. Otherwise, proceed with payment based on documented market rates.”

“If this is your final position, provide it in writing with policy citations. I will escalate to management and DOI if unresolved.”
