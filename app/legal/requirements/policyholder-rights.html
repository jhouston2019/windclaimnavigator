Policyholder Rights Overview

Core legal protections for notice, communication, prompt payment, appeal options, and freedom from retaliation.

On This Page

    Overview

    Policyholders are protected by state unfair claims acts, prompt-payment statutes, and policy duties of good faith. You have rights to timely responses, clear explanations, fair investigation, and payment of what is owed without harassment or retaliation.

    Key Rules & Requirements

    • Timely communication: Carriers must acknowledge claims and respond to inquiries within statutory deadlines.
    • Fair investigation: Adjusters must conduct a thorough, unbiased investigation before denying claims.
    • Written decisions: Denials and reservations must cite policy provisions and factual bases.
    • Payment of undisputed benefits: Even if parts are disputed, undisputed amounts must be paid promptly.
    • Appeal/complaint rights: You can escalate internally and file complaints with the state regulator without penalty.
    • Anti-retaliation: Carriers cannot cancel or non-renew solely because you file a claim or complaint.

    Step-by-Step Guidance

    1. Ask for all deadlines in writing (acknowledgment, inspection, decision, payment).
    2. Request written coverage positions with specific policy citations.
    3. Track all communications in a claim log with dates and commitments.
    4. If deadlines are missed, send a statutory reminder and request supervisor review.
    5. Escalate unresolved issues to the state department of insurance and note complaint numbers.
    6. Consult counsel before suit limitations expire if the claim remains unpaid or unfairly denied.

    Required Documentation

    • Policy documents and declarations page.
    • Communication log and copies of emails/letters.
    • Adjuster reports, estimates, and decision letters.
    • Proof of loss, receipts, and evidence supporting your claim.
    • Complaint filings or escalation letters, if any.

    Common Insurer Tactics

    • Providing verbal decisions without written support.
    • Delaying investigations through serial document requests.
    • Failing to separate undisputed amounts from disputed items.
    • Using broad exclusions without analyzing specific facts.

    Red Flags to Watch For

    • No acknowledgment or status updates within required timeframes.
    • Denials lacking policy citations or factual detail.
    • Pressure to accept low settlements without justification.
    • Threats of cancellation or non-renewal related to claim activity.

    Best Practices for Policyholders

    • Keep everything in writing; confirm phone calls by email.
    • Quote state statutes when following up on missed deadlines.
    • Request undisputed payments early and often.
    • Escalate respectfully but firmly; involve supervisors and regulators when needed.
    • Monitor suit limitations and seek advice before they expire.
