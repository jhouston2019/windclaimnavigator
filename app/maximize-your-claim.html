Overview

Build a claim that demands full value

Purpose: give you the exact documentation, valuation, and timing moves that stop underpayment and keep the carrier on track.

Why claims get undervalued

  • Gaps in documentation or proof of loss
  • Understated scope, pricing, or depreciation
  • Poor sequencing or slow responses to carrier requests

The three drivers of full payout

  • Documentation: complete, organized, and mapped to policy obligations
  • Valuation: scope accuracy, market-rate pricing, and fair depreciation
  • Timing: correct sequence with responsive communication

Must-haves

  • Proper proof of loss
  • Damage documentation
  • Accurate valuation
  • Responsive communication
  • Correct sequencing

Valuation

Claim Value Factors

Align scope, pricing, and coverage so every line is defensible.

Scope Accuracy

  • Room-by-room quantities with photos
  • Include code, permits, and overhead
  • Note concealed/latent damage risk

Depreciation Application

  • Use age/condition evidence to limit holdback
  • Challenge arbitrary depreciation tables
  • Track recoverable depreciation triggers

Labor & Material Pricing Accuracy

  • Local market rates, seasonality, surge pricing
  • Realistic production rates by trade
  • Manufacturer specs and install standards

Coverage Interpretation

  • Map line items to policy language
  • Apply endorsements and supplemental coverages
  • Document causation and exclusions responses

Supplemental Claim Opportunities

  • Price changes, code upgrades, scope gaps
  • Denied items with new evidence
  • Additional living expense extensions

Timing & Sequence

Execute in the right order

Keep momentum with crisp submissions, tracked responses, and structured follow-ups.

1

Initial Documentation

Report the loss, open claim file, capture photos/video, and log mitigation actions.

2

Evidence Submission

Submit proof of loss, core documents, and organized evidence package tied to the policy.

3

Carrier Response

Track adjuster requests, deadlines, and inspection dates in a single communication log.

4

Estimate Review & Disputes

Compare scopes, challenge pricing/depreciation, and submit supplements with supporting evidence.

5

Supplemental or Appeal

Escalate with formal disputes, policy citations, and updated evidence packages as needed.

Risk Control

Avoid These Mistakes

Preventable errors that commonly reduce payouts or slow approvals.

Missing key documents

  • No proof of loss or incomplete receipts
  • Policy excerpts not attached to disputes

Submitting evidence too early or too late

  • Scope submitted before mitigation complete
  • Late responses leading to delays

Inconsistencies in explanations

  • Mismatched quantities between forms and estimates
  • Changing causation narratives without support

Allowing communication gaps

  • Untracked phone calls or verbal approvals
  • No written follow-up confirming commitments

Not tracking inspections

  • Missing attendance by your representative
  • No photo log of inspection findings

Accepting a lowball without dispute

  • No written counter with evidence
  • Leaving supplements or appeals unused
