Carrier Communication Letters

Use formal letters to document expectations and commitments with the carrier.

Professional tone Documentation Follow-ups

Overview

Purpose

  • Reference claim number, policy, adjuster name, and recent activity.
  • State the purpose of the letter and the action or answer you need.
  • Keep tone professional and outline next steps with dates.

Quality checklist

Ready-to-send

  • Include contact info and preferred response channel.
  • Attach or reference supporting documents cited in the letter.
  • Provide a clear deadline and note if regulatory timelines apply.

What to include

Use these pieces to keep the carrier communication letters packet complete.

Core documents

Build the file

  • Initial engagement or acknowledgement letter to the adjuster.
  • Follow-up letter summarizing calls and pending items.
  • Escalation letter to a supervisor with a concise issue list.

Supporting proof

Evidence to attach

  • Call notes with dates and attendees.
  • Copies of prior submissions or portal confirmations.
  • Screenshots or PDFs of referenced materials.

Delivery notes

Keep it moving

  • Send as a signed PDF via email and upload to any carrier portal.
  • CC the insured or counsel when appropriate.
  • Log delivery time and promised response dates in the communication log.
