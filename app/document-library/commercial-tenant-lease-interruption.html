Commercial Tenant Lease Interruption

Document lease terms, notices, and rent impacts for affected tenants.

Lease terms Rent loss Notices

Overview

Purpose

  • Identify applicable lease clauses for interruption or force majeure.
  • Explain operational impacts and rent abatements or offsets.
  • Capture communications between landlord and tenant.

Quality checklist

Ready-to-send

  • Include rent schedule, escalation clauses, and amounts claimed.
  • Note mitigation or temporary space arrangements.
  • Follow notice requirements and delivery methods in the lease.

What to include

Use these pieces to keep the commercial tenant lease interruption packet complete.

Core documents

Build the file

  • Executed lease and amendments.
  • Notice letters about interruption and access limitations.
  • Rent ledger before and after the loss.

Supporting proof

Evidence to attach

  • Photos showing inaccessible or damaged areas.
  • City closure orders, inspection tags, or safety notices.
  • Invoices for temporary space or alternative arrangements.

Delivery notes

Keep it moving

  • Send notices by the method required in the lease and keep proof.
  • Track acknowledgment from counterparties.
  • Align timelines with lease cure or response periods.
