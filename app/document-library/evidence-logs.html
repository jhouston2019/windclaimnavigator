Evidence Logs

Track when evidence is collected, transferred, and received.

Chain of custody Tracking Audit

Overview

Purpose

  • Log each piece of evidence with source, date, and custodian.
  • Record where items are stored and who has access.
  • Note submissions to carriers or experts with timestamps.

Quality checklist

Ready-to-send

  • Time-stamp every entry.
  • Attach proof of transfer or receipt.
  • Note retention or destruction requirements.

What to include

Use these pieces to keep the evidence logs packet complete.

Core documents

Build the file

  • Evidence log sheet or database export.
  • Submission receipts or shipping labels.
  • Custodian contact list.

Supporting proof

Evidence to attach

  • Photos of sealed packaging for physical evidence.
  • Email confirmations of receipt.
  • Inventory lists for samples or materials.

Delivery notes

Keep it moving

  • Export the log when sharing exhibits in disputes.
  • Keep a backup copy separate from the working file.
  • Review the log monthly for completeness.
