Fire Damage Documents

Capture origin, spread, safety, and restoration details.

Fire Safety Restoration

Overview

Purpose

  • Document origin and cause findings when available.
  • Show soot, smoke, and water damage with clear labeling.
  • Track remediation steps and clearance milestones.

Quality checklist

Ready-to-send

  • Include fire reports and investigator notes.
  • Identify salvageable versus total loss items.
  • Record cleaning, deodorization, and testing results.

What to include

Use these pieces to keep the fire damage documents packet complete.

Core documents

Build the file

  • Fire department or investigator report.
  • Inventory of damaged structure and contents.
  • Restoration scopes and contractor estimates.

Supporting proof

Evidence to attach

  • Air quality or soot sampling results.
  • Before-and-after photos.
  • Temporary housing or emergency service invoices.

Delivery notes

Keep it moving

  • Organize by structure and contents folders.
  • Highlight any safety clearances or re-entry approvals.
  • Provide contact details for restoration vendors.
